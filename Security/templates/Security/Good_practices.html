Social Media

To be honest a lot of people reveal way too much info about themselves on social media platforms.For instance home address, email addresses and phone numbers. These can all be extracted by automatic programs and are often sold to companies or others with ill intentions.Then they use this information to send you spam mail or give you calls to sell products and in other cases even used for illegal activity.So try to minimize this info you put out there. Why do people need to know where you stay or what your phone number is if it's not a close friend or work colleague.Same with email addresses Don't just paste them anywhere.Understandably you do need to register on sites for some services occasionally, Just be sure the site is legit and if you want to be extra careful use a service like Guerrilla Mail which is a temporary email service.

Downloads

Downloads along with emails are the main source of virus acquisition be sure to download programs with certificates that your browser trusts, Some legitimate programs do have certification problems and are still valid programs.When downloading these just be sure to have your antivirus scan the program before downloading.

Visiting sites

When visiting websites certain precautions should be taken to ensure your safety and the safety of your personal details on the site...Here are a few things to look out for.In your search bar where the address is displayed check for the 'S' in https, The 'S' is short for secure and without it any data entered into forms is up for grabs by unwanted parties! Also if there's a privacy policy somewhere that's also a good sign.There should also be a little lock on the far left of your search bar.And then the pop ups...Don't click on pop ups stating that you have won something or anything trying to entice you to leave the particular site.

Emails

Do not open attachments on emails from people/entities you don't know.Especially if the attachment is media related or the content seems to lure you to open the attachment.A virus is quickly transferred to your home pc or business with an attachment like this.So just be careful.Your antivirus should scan incoming emails though.But it's still a very real threat.

Passwords

In terms of passwords, use something more than 10 characters long, be sure to use upper and lower case.Include a digit or two...preferably not 69.But that is an awesome password though.Also include some symbols if allowed and that is it. That's a pretty secure password that's not easily cracked
