Settings to change on Desktop

Home

Here are some classes of settings that you can change on your desktop.Some apps like maps will need to use these settings...But it is safer to have it in the off position and if a certain app really needs to use it, It will inform you of it.But in my case that's never happened.

Privacy settings

From your desktop view after startup click on start/Windows icon and then on the settings tab then click on Privacy from there change your settings to the examples. I will provide short descriptions as to why. Most are self explanatory.

General

Camera

Switch off the Camera, Unless you are actively using it.There are people out there that know how to access it. plus some apps you download you actually unknowingly give permission to access your device camera.Meaning they can look through it whenever they please.

Location

So deactivating this tab will allow you to hide your location from any unwanted 'visitors',This is a safe thing to do. Why would you want potential strangers or companies to know your exact location within a few meters. off with the switch!

Microphone

The microphone.If you are not using it for voice searches or maybe skype then switch iit off.There are people that know how to listen in on your personal conversations.

Account info

Switch it off.Why would someone want that info from you.The less they know about you the better.

Contacts

Your contacts are YOUR contacts.No one elses..Shut it down.

Calender

Want complete strangers to know your birthday or any potential meetings or events you have set up? NO.Switch it off

Messaging

Leave our messages alone.Off.

Radios

Switch it to the OFF position.

Wifi-sense

This feature can actually be used to share your password on other networks switch it off like this, From WiFi go to manage WiFi settings

Windows media player

Copy the settings below, They are pretty self explanatory

Patching

This is the single most important feature.Keep your security up to date as there is a constant evolving of new malicious Software being developed.Head over to the Software requirements page to see how you can automate this process

Users

It is good practice to run your system on a different account than the administrator account. If someone were to gain access to your system, this would severely limit the damage they can cause as they will only be able to perform actions under the standard user. So add a standard user account and work with that.
